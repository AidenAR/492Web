Accessible CAPTCHA Alternatives

Traditional CAPTCHAs often present accessibility challenges for users with disabilities. In our research, we have explored several alternative approaches that provide security without relying on visually or audibly demanding tasks. Some promising alternatives include:

  • Anti-spam honeypots: Hidden fields or traps that bots may fill out, flagging them as automated.
  • Bot protection software: Specialized services that detect non-human behavior using sophisticated algorithms.
  • Web Application Firewalls (WAF): Filtering and monitoring of HTTP traffic to prevent malicious activity.
  • Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA): Adding extra layers of security that go beyond simple CAPTCHA challenges.
  • Biometric security: Utilizing fingerprint, facial, or voice recognition, where applicable.
  • Behavior-based analysis: Monitoring user interactions such as mouse movements and click patterns to distinguish bots from humans.

These alternatives offer different advantages and drawbacks in terms of accessibility, privacy, and security. Our project examines each method to propose inclusive solutions that protect websites while ensuring every user can access services without barriers.
